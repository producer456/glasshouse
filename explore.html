The idea

Most privacy advice is abstract. Glasshouse explains a thing, then demonstrates it on you — so it stops being theoretical. Tap each Show me button below. Everything happens locally; this page makes no network calls of its own.

1 · Your browser has a unique signature

You don't need cookies to be tracked. The exact mix of your screen, fonts, GPU, timezone and settings is often unique enough to single you out across sites — silently. Here's yours, computed live:

2 · The invisible canvas test

Sites can ask your browser to draw a hidden image, then read it back. Tiny differences in your GPU/driver/OS can make the result reliably you — a classic cookieless trick. It works fully in Chrome; Safari, Firefox and Brave now add noise to defeat it, so on those you may get a different result each run. Let's see yours:

🧒 Like you're 5: We ask your computer to doodle a tiny picture. It draws it just a little differently than everyone else — like handwriting. So the doodle alone whispers "this is you."

4 · The local-network leak (WebRTC)

The WebRTC feature used for video calls once leaked your device's local network address. Most current browsers now mask it as a random .local name, so a raw-IP leak is the uncommon, misconfigured case today (and this only ever exposed your local address, not your public/VPN IP).
